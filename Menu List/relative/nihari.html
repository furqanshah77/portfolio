Nihari

Nihari is a popular and flavorful slow-cooked stew that is widely enjoyed in Pakistan. It is a rich and hearty dish known for its tender meat, aromatic spices, and thick gravy. Nihari is typically made with beef or mutton, which is simmered for hours to achieve a melt-in-your-mouth texture and develop a robust flavor.

The name "Nihari" is derived from the Arabic word "Nahar," which means "morning." Traditionally, Nihari was prepared overnight and consumed as a nourishing breakfast to provide energy for the day. However, it is now enjoyed throughout the day as a filling and indulgent meal.
